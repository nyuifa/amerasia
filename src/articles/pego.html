Pego

Pego, today spelled Bago, was the capital of Myanmar from 1369-1538. Numerous sixteenth-century maps, including Giacomo Gastaldi’s woodcut map Cosmographia universalis (c. 1561) and Giuseppe Rosaccio’s copper engraving Universale descrittione (1597) showed the Burmese capital (Pagul) in what is today North America, even after European cartographers began to separate the continents with a strait or an ocean.
