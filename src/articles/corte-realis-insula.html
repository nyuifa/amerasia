Corte Realis Insula

Vopel has named this large island off the coast of his Amerasian continent Corte Realis insula. The Portuguese explorer Gaspar Corte-Real, together with his father João Vaz Corte-Real and his brother Miguel, undertook a variety of exploratory voyages sponsored by the Portuguese Crown, including some of the first to reach what is today Newfoundland, as well as other parts of northeastern Canada.

In the preface to his 1502 translation of Marco Polo, which he addressed directly to King John II, the Portuguese translator Valentim Fernandes proclaimed that the recent Corte-Real voyages had joined together Orient and Occident.

Even to the ultimate ends of the West and the beginnings of the East, all the way to the lands of the Great Khan (where already your powerful name [King John II] begins to resound), where lie the very noble provinces of Tenduch, Mangy, Tanguth, etc., the beginning of which, according to my limited knowledge, was discovered by the most honorable gentlemen Gaspar Corte-Real.[1]

Fernandes understood Columbus’, Corte-Real's, and Polo’s Indies to be one and the same, and in discussing them depicted an Amerasian Portuguese empire.

[1] “[C]omeço de oriente ate as terra do gram Cham onde ja começa de soar vosso poderoso nomem onde jazem as my nobres provicias Tencudh, Mangy, Tanguth etc. o principio das quaes Segundo o meu pequeno saber achou o muy honrrado fidalguo Gaspar Corte Real,” Valentim Fernandes, O Livro de Marco Paulo, Lisbon 1502, ed. Francisco Maria Esteves Pereira (Lisbon: Biblioteca Nacional, 1922), Sign. A.ij (v).
