London

Richard Eden’s A Treatyse of the Newe India, published in London in 1553, was the first printed English text to consider the voyages of Columbus and Vespucci, sixty years after they first occurred. By the time Eden published his Treatyse, European ideas about Amerasia had been developing in Europe for over fifty years. His text transmitted previously published Amerasian representations, in large part by including a translation of Sebastian Münster's Cosmographia (Basel, 1550), the first German language description of the world, which expressed a clear Amerasian understanding.[1] Book Five of the Cosmographia, which Eden translated to form the body of his Treatyse, grouped Asia and America together as one unified part of the world, making America part of an archipelago that stretched from the Canaries to the Moluccas.
